Gnoll Encounter

Preface

This game is an attempt to recreate an encouter with a Goblin in the format of Dungeons and Dragons Fifth Edition (D&D5E). Dungeons and Dragons is as much about storytelling as it is about the odds. The JavaScript program that I've created follows the basic rules of D&D5E, such as assigning you an armor class, hit points, and an attack that deals a randomly determined amount of damage based on dice rolls. Don't worry, though! You don't have to do any of the math and tracking that normally comes with D&D to play this game. All you have to do is relax, read the story, and roll the dice!

How to Play


There will be a small paragraph of story presented with each option.

Story text will appear like this.

There may also be directions from the Dungeon Master. In a normal Dungeons and Dragons game, the Dungeon Master is another person who controls the direction of the game and the non-playable-characters that you encounter. In this game, the program is the Dungeon Master, and has some predetermined dialogue that you can explore!

Directions and quotations from the dungeon master will appear like this.

Quick Rule Rundown

  • During this short game, you will take turns rolling dice for the opportunity to deal damage to your opponent.
  • For simplicity, we will not worry about Initiative this game.
  • Attack rolls are made against your opponent's Armor Class, a number that you have to beat in order to roll for damage.
  • The game is over when either you, or your opponent's, Hit Points are reduced to zero.
